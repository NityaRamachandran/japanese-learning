Japanese Letters

There are totally 3 types of scripts used to read and write the japanese language

  • Hiragana 平仮名
  • katakana 片仮名
  • Kanji 漢字

The Hiragana and Katakana are letters, whereas, the Kanji are characters each having their meaning as words

The term kanji in Japanese literally means "Han characters". It is written in Japanese by using the same characters as in traditional Chinese, and both refer to the character writing system known in Chinese as hanzi

There is no such pronounciation as "L" in japanese. The "R" sound is used for both.
